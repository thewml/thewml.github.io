Organizing a "Lecture" in a Tree + Having a page flow.

  • From: Shlomi Fish <nospam@thanx>
  • Date: Wed, 19 Sep 2001 13:00:34 +0200 (IST)

Hi!

I wrote a tool called QuaD-Pres
(http://vipe.technion.ac.il/~shlomif/quadpres/) in Perl, which I used to
assist me in creating HTML lectures. Now, the main feature of Quad-Pres
is the ability to organize the lecture in a tree and to present
latex2html-like "Next", "Prev" and "Up" buttons. It also has the ability
to present the sub-tree emerging from the current node.

Now, I learned WML only later, and discovered it had many features not
present in Quad-Pres, which may prove useful instead of working with Raw
HTML. However, if it doesn't has the features I just described, I'll have
to stick with Quad-Pres or (better yet) port the relevant Quad-Pres APIs  
to work with WML.

Does anybody know of any pre-coded and/or standard solution that exists
for WML?

For your reference here are some lectures I prepared with Quad-Pres (in
order of preparation):

http://vipe.technion.ac.il/~shlomif/lecture/Perl/Newbies/lecture1/
http://vipe.technion.ac.il/~shlomif/lecture/Perl/Newbies/lecture2/
http://vipe.technion.ac.il/~shlomif/lecture/Gimp/1/slides/
http://vipe.technion.ac.il/~shlomif/lecture/Perl/Newbies/lecture3/

The latter is not completely finished, and the Gimp one lacks some
material in the middle. I'd like to fix it when I have some time, but it
is still usable as it is.
