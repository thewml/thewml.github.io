Ditto that on the short tags. They should be kept, and I love how the
redirection tags are almost like a little pictorial of what they mean.
"..TAG>> <<.."

Anyway, check out the wml::std::tags include file. It translates the non
SGML compliant tags into nice SGML compliant forms if that is your thing.
For example, the following tags are defined:

-----
<dump NAME>
<enter NAME> ... <leave>
<divert NAME> ... </divert>
-----

Opps, the <enter> .. <leave> pair is not standard (I don' think), but that
could be easily changed to <enter NAME> ... </enter>.
Check out the man page on that include file at:
     http://www.engelschall.com/sw/wml/docs/incl/wml::std::tags.html
