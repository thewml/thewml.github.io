       i tried

#use wml::all

       once with wml-1.6.8 and it failed. i thought it was something
       with my messy installation.
       now, with wml-1.7.1 the same thing happens again. then i
       realized that i actually have all.wml file in lib/include but
       wmk looks in lib/include/usr, which does not exist. i just
       created taht directory and linked all.wml and #use wml::all
       works good now.

       so, it could be a bug in the installation procedure.
